📊 THE 10 FACTORS AT A GLANCE

┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                     TIER 1: THE BIG THREE                                 │
│                  (30-50% Effect Each - Non-Negotiable)                     │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  ✅ 1. pH (3.0 - 9.0)
     Effect:    30-40% capacity variance
     Why:       Bell curve, optimal at pH 6.5
     Mechanism: Controls surface charge, ion exchange mechanism
     Papers:    40/40 test this (100%)
     Delta:     ±0.5 pH = ±5-10% efficiency change

  ✅ 2. Initial Concentration C₀ (1 - 10 mg/L)
     Effect:    20-50% variance (Langmuir isotherm)
     Why:       Fundamental driving force, shows saturation
     Mechanism: Defines adsorption equilibrium relationship
     Papers:    40/40 test this (100%)
     Delta:     2→4 mg/L = -10-15% removal

  ✅ 3. Contact Time (10 - 120 minutes)
     Effect:    70% at 30 min → 95% at 120 min
     Why:       Biggest Langmuir gap (assumes instant equilibrium)
     Mechanism: Pseudo-second-order kinetics, approach to saturation
     Papers:    38/40 test this (95%)
     Delta:     30→60 min = +15-20% capacity gain

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                  TIER 2: MAJOR PROCESS VARIABLES                          │
│              (5-40% Effect - Practical Importance)                         │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  ✅ 4. Adsorbent Dose (0.5 - 5.0 g/L)
     Effect:    30-40% linear increase
     Why:       Practical question: How much adsorbent needed?
     Mechanism: Site availability, interactions with pH & time
     Papers:    30/40 test this (75%)
     Delta:     0.5→1.0 g/L = 50% capacity change
     Added:     YES (now possible with unlimited compute)

  ✅ 5. Temperature (20 - 40°C)
     Effect:    5-10% per 20°C increase (Arrhenius exponential)
     Why:       Real water varies seasonally, validates kinetics
     Mechanism: Activation energy (~20 kJ/mol), K_L temperature correction
     Papers:    25/40 test this (63%)
     Delta:     5°C change = 2-3% efficiency increase
     Added:     YES (validates E_a)

  ✅ 6. Flow Rate (0.5 - 2.0 L/min)
     Effect:    20-30% efficiency change (inverse relationship)
     Why:       Critical for column operation (practical application)
     Mechanism: Residence time → contact time effect
     Papers:    12/40 test this (30% - column studies only)
     Delta:     0.5→1.5 L/min = -20-25% removal
     Added:     (Already in 5-factor)

  ✅ 7. Competing Anions - Chloride (0 - 100 mg/L)
     Effect:    15-30% capacity reduction
     Why:       Bridge to field applicability (real groundwater has Cl⁻)
     Mechanism: Ion exchange competition for adsorption sites
     Papers:    15/40 test this (37% specifically)
     Delta:     50→100 mg/L Cl⁻ = -10-15% removal
     Added:     YES (real water chemistry)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                TIER 3: REAL-WORLD GROUNDWATER CHEMISTRY                   │
│              (5-30% Effect - Field Applicability)                         │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  ✅ 8. Water Hardness (0 - 500 mg/L as CaCO₃)
     Effect:    5-15% capacity reduction
     Why:       Every natural groundwater has hardness
     Mechanism: Divalent cation (Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺) competition + precipitation
     Papers:    8/40 test this specifically
     Delta:     Soft (50)→Hard (300) = -8-12% removal
     Added:     YES (universal in all groundwater)

  ✅ 9. Carbonate (0 - 100 mg/L as HCO₃⁻)
     Effect:    20-30% reduction at high concentrations
     Why:       STRONGEST competing anion (not just Cl⁻)
     Mechanism: Carbonate species competition + pH buffering effects
     Papers:    15/40 test this (~37%)
     Delta:     30→80 mg/L CO₃²⁻ = -15-20% removal
     Added:     YES (geochemically important in limestone regions)

  ✅ 10. Natural Organic Matter - Humic Acid (0 - 50 mg/L)
      Effect:    10-20% capacity reduction through fouling
      Why:       Real water degrades performance (surface coating)
      Mechanism: Humic acids coat surface, block active sites
      Papers:    3-4/40 test this (rare, growing area)
      Delta:     0→30 mg/L NOM = -12-18% removal
      Added:     YES (fouling mechanism, real degradation)
