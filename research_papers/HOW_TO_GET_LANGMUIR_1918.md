# How to Get the Original Langmuir 1918 Paper

## The Paper
**Title:** "The Adsorption of Gases on Plane Surfaces of Glass, Mica and Platinum"  
**Author:** Irving Langmuir  
**Year:** 1918  
**Journal:** Journal of the American Chemical Society, Vol. 40(9), pp. 1361–1403  
**DOI:** 10.1021/ja02242a004  
**Status:** ✅ **PUBLIC DOMAIN** — copyright has expired

---

## ✅ Fastest Way (2 clicks)

### Option 1: Google Scholar (Recommended - Easiest)
1. Go to **https://scholar.google.com**
2. Search: `Langmuir adsorption gases 1918`
3. Look for: "The adsorption of gases on plane surfaces..."
4. Click **[PDF]** link on the right side
5. **Done!** — automatically downloads

---

### Option 2: Direct Journal Link (Official Source)
1. Go to: **https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/ja02242a004**
2. Scroll down
3. Click **"Read Online"** or **"View PDF"** button
4. PDF opens (may require clicking through one page)

---

### Option 3: Internet Archive (Archive.org)
1. Go to: **https://archive.org/search.php?query=langmuir+adsorption+1918**
2. Find the paper in results
3. Click **"Borrow"** or **"Read Online"**
4. Full text available

---

### Option 4: Your University Library
1. Go to your university library website
2. Search by DOI: `10.1021/ja02242a004`
3. Usually one-click access for institutional members

---

## Why Not Automated Download?

Journal websites intentionally block automated downloads to prevent abuse. But you can always access the paper manually — it takes ~30 seconds:

1. **Google Scholar** → search title
2. **Click [PDF]** link
3. **Save As** to your computer

This is the standard way researchers access all papers, free or paywalled.

## Key Content from the Paper

The paper introduces the **Langmuir Isotherm equation** used throughout your project:

$$q_e = \frac{q_{max} \cdot K_L \cdot C_e}{1 + K_L \cdot C_e}$$

Where:
- $q_e$ = amount adsorbed at equilibrium
- $q_{max}$ = monolayer saturation capacity
- $K_L$ = Langmuir adsorption constant
- $C_e$ = equilibrium concentration

This foundational work is the basis for your entire Phase 2 model.
